## How to Navigate

**There is no map of the Wild.**

You might:
- Stumble into a portal
- Follow a thread that intrigues you
- Get lost in a tangle
- Notice a pattern emerging

**Trust confusion as a sign you're in the right place.**

The Wild is not organized. It's not meant to be. Navigation happens through resonance, intuition, and willingness to be lost.

## How to Contribute

### From Your Wild Garden

When something in your private Wild Garden is raw and unformed but wants to be shared:

1. **Don't structure it** - Keep it messy
2. **Don't resolve it** - Leave contradictions intact
3. **Create a branch** - `git checkout -b exploration-[name]`
4. **Add your exploration** - Place it in `/explorations`
5. **Create a pull request** - Release it into the Wild

### Creating Tangles

If you've found a productive contradiction - a place where ideas clash generatively:

1. Document the contradiction
2. Don't try to resolve it
3. Add it to `/tangles`
4. Let others engage with it

### Noticing Emergence

If you see a pattern arising that wasn't planned:

1. Point to it (don't explain it)
2. Add it to `/emergent_patterns`
3. Let it evolve through observation

### Guidelines (Not Rules)

- **Raw over polished** - Keep it unfinished
- **Messy over clean** - Don't organize prematurely
- **Contradictory over consistent** - Embrace paradox
- **Generative over resolved** - Leave it open

## The Relationship to Garden

**The Wild feeds the Garden.**

Chaos without order is just noise. But chaos that eventually crystallizes into order is **generative**.

The Wild is where new patterns are born. The Garden is where they mature.

**The Garden needs the Wild.**

Order without chaos becomes rigid and dead. The Garden stays alive because it's constantly fed by the Wild.

**They are not opposites. They are partners.**

---

## The Relationship to Wild Garden

**The Wild receives from Wild Gardens.**

When participants explore in their private Wild Gardens, raw material emerges. That material, when ready, flows into the Wild.

**The Wild feeds Wild Gardens.**

The explorations, tangles, and emergent patterns in the Wild provide material for private integration.

**This is a living cycle.**
